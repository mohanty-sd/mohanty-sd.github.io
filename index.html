About

(This entire site is under construction.) I am a Professor in the Department of Physics and Astronomy and a member of the Center for Gravitational Wave Astronomy (CGWA) at the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley. My research focuses on gravitational wave data analysis, swarm intelligence methods, and statistical techniques for detecting signals from astrophysical sources.

With over two decades of experience in gravitational wave astronomy, I have contributed significantly to the field through my work on efficient data analysis techniques, particle swarm optimization methods, and the development of novel algorithms for detecting gravitational wave signals from various astrophysical sources including binary black holes, neutron stars, and extreme mass ratio inspirals.

I am also actively involved in international collaborations, serving on advisory committees for major gravitational wave projects including the Tianqin space-based detector project in China, and have extensive experience in supervising graduate students and postdoctoral researchers.

Research Interests

Gravitational Wave Data Analysis

Development of efficient algorithms and techniques for detecting gravitational wave signals from various astrophysical sources including binary black holes, neutron stars, and extreme mass ratio inspirals.

Swarm Intelligence Methods

Application of particle swarm optimization and other swarm intelligence techniques to statistical regression, signal processing, and parameter estimation problems in astronomy.

Space-based Gravitational Wave Detectors

Research on data analysis methods for future space-based gravitational wave missions including LISA and the Chinese Tianqin project.

Pulsar Timing Arrays

Development of methods for detecting gravitational waves using pulsar timing array data and multi-messenger astronomy approaches.

Machine Learning in Astronomy

Application of deep learning and artificial intelligence techniques to astronomical data analysis, including glitch detection in gravitational wave data.

Computational Physics

High-performance computing applications in gravitational wave astronomy and development of GPU-accelerated algorithms.
